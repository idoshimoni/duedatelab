Chinese Gender Chart

How the chart works

The traditional chart is organized by the mother's lunar age at conception and the lunar month of conception. Lunar age adds roughly one year to your Western age, because Chinese tradition counts the year in the womb as age one.

Legend says it was found in a royal tomb over 700 years ago. Studies that checked it against real birth outcomes put its accuracy at about 50%, i.e. no better than a coin flip. It's a fun tool, not a diagnostic.

Want a real answer?

An ultrasound after 18 weeks, or non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT) from 10 weeks, can tell you the actual sex of your baby. After birth, see how your baby compares on our growth percentile chart.

Sources & accuracy

  • Villamor E, et al. "Accuracy of the Chinese lunar calendar method to predict a baby's sex." J Reprod Med, 2010.

The only peer-reviewed study concluded the chart performs no better than chance (~50%). This tool is a novelty; for real answers use an 18-week ultrasound or NIPT from week 10.
