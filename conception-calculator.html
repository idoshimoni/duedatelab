How we estimate conception

Conception usually happens within a 6-day fertile window that ends on the day of ovulation, typically about 14 days before your next period (Wilcox et al. 1995). To project forward from there, use our Due Date Calculator. For a 28-day cycle that's day 14. We adjust for longer or shorter cycles so the estimate makes sense for you.

For ultrasound dating, we work backward from the gestational age the scan measured. For IVF, conception is the day the egg was fertilized: 5 days before a blastocyst transfer, 3 days before a day-3 transfer. After birth, the Baby Percentile Calculator shows where your baby lands on WHO growth curves.

Frequently asked questions

How is conception date estimated?

For a regular cycle, conception is estimated as ovulation day, roughly 14 days before the next expected period. A 28-day cycle puts conception around day 14; a 32-day cycle pushes it to around day 18. The calculator uses your cycle length to land on a narrow date range rather than a single exact day.

Can you calculate conception for IVF?

Yes. Conception is the known fertilization date, which is 5 days before a Day-5 blastocyst transfer and 3 days before a Day-3 embryo transfer. Because IVF timing is observed directly, the resulting conception date is more precise than a natural-cycle estimate and is what your fertility clinic will use for dating.

What is the fertile window?

The fertile window is a six-day span that ends on ovulation day (Wilcox et al. 1995). Sperm can survive in the reproductive tract for up to five days, so conception can result from intercourse in the days leading up to ovulation. The two days before and including ovulation carry the highest chance of conception.

Can an ultrasound tell me the exact conception date?

An early ultrasound measures the fetus and estimates gestational age in weeks and days. We work backward from that measurement to infer conception. It is the most accurate non-IVF method, especially in the first trimester. After about 14 weeks, fetal size varies more between healthy pregnancies, so the estimate loses precision.

Is conception the same as implantation?

No. Conception, also called fertilization, is when the sperm and egg join and happens within about 24 hours of ovulation. Implantation is when the resulting embryo attaches to the uterine lining and happens six to twelve days later. Pregnancy tests detect the hormone hCG, which is only produced after implantation.

How does cycle length change the conception date?

Cycle length shifts ovulation. The luteal phase from ovulation to the next period is roughly fixed at 14 days, so a longer cycle means ovulation and conception happen later in the cycle, and a shorter cycle means earlier. The calculator accepts cycle length between 20 and 45 days and applies this shift automatically.
